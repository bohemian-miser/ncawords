Interactive demo

Two Organisms, One World

Two independently-trained Growing-NCA organisms — say a lizard and a spiderweb — share one 16-channel grid. A control field B, painted by hand, chooses per cell which organism’s learned update rule runs. Seeds planted in each region grow their own creature; at painted boundaries the two rule-sets negotiate.

Each organism is a Growing Neural Cellular Automaton: a tiny network, trained end-to-end, that reads a cell’s 3×3 neighborhood (identity + Sobel filters over all 16 channels) and outputs a state update dx. Normally one such rule governs the whole grid. Here both rules are evaluated at every cell on the exact same shared state, and the painted field B mixes their outputs:

dx = (1 − B) · dxA + B · dxB

Where B ≈ 0 the first organism’s physics applies; where B ≈ 1 the second’s. Neither network was ever trained to see the other — whatever happens at a painted boundary is pure negotiation between two rule-sets that each think they own the world.

The default organisms here (tough-*, and the firm-* alternatives in the dropdowns) are hardened for exactly this kind of boundary fight: training hits them with heavier, more frequent damage than the original vanilla-* runs, then makes them survive long no-grad “fester” rollouts — many extra steps with no gradient update — before grading resumes, so the learned rule has to hold its shape over a much longer horizon. That should make the shared-grid negotiation more stable at painted boundaries than the original vanilla weights. The hardened runs are still training as this is written; if a tough-* selection 404s, the demo automatically falls back to that organism’s vanilla-* weights and says so below the canvas.

Organism weights are read live from a public training bucket, the same source the experiment gallery uses. If a model is still training its weights may not exist yet — retry in a little while. tough-* selections that aren’t exported yet fall back automatically to that organism’s vanilla-* weights in the meantime.
