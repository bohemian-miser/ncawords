NCA Research Log

A running log of experiments beyond the original text-growing models — dynamics analysis, emergent machinery, and learning cellular-automata rules.

Criticality is a training attractor

We probed 221 trained models with a spectral-radius + Lyapunov battery. Canonical growth models sit right at criticality (spectral radius ≈ 1.0), but the best performers are slightly supercritical (1.01–1.27) while collapsed or underfit models sit exactly at 1.000.

Objective “exoticness” tracks it: simple letter-growth models cluster around 1.01, staged models around 1.15, organic models around 1.18, and slime models around 1.23 (and strongly chaotic), rising to 1.39 for Hilbert curves and 1.51 for Spectre tilings.

A tuned fire-rate optimum

Cells update stochastically; the field’s default is a 50% fire rate per step. A sweep found a shallow but real optimum around 0.70–0.75 (loss ≈ 0.0226, versus ≈ 0.026 at the extremes of the sweep).

The spectral radius stayed pinned near 1.0 across every rate tested — so the optimum is independent of criticality.

Emergent self-scaffolding (the success cloud)

Letters normally need a pre-drawn scaffold to grow in the right places; without one, growth collapses to a single letter. We gave formed letters a diffusing “success cloud” that neighbors read as a positional signal, plus a thin living mist so the cloud survives in empty space.

With it, the word COMP grows fully from a bare seed with no scaffold at all.

Colour-free training invents its own palette

Supervising only the alpha/activation channel and leaving RGB completely free, the model still invents structured, per-letter colouring as a byproduct of its internal coordination — nothing in the loss asks for colour at all.

Learning the rules of Conway’s Game of Life

Toward “fixed rules, trained machinery”: a neural CA can learn Life exactly, but only with the right perception. A neighbour-count (sum) filter reaches near-perfect prediction (cross-entropy 0.0003), while the standard Sobel-gradient perception fails completely (0.52) — you cannot recover a neighbour count from directional gradients.

Experiment 2: machinery inside frozen rules In progress

With Life learned, we freeze the physics and instead train an initial board so that a masked target behaviour emerges — the free cells become whatever machinery is needed to make it happen.

Key obstacle found: a faithful rule-learner is too saturated to backprop through, and naive optimisation is caught between a flat zero-gradient empty board and chaotic soup. A temperature-annealed soft-Life plus a horizon curriculum makes it trainable — a still-life sanity target already reaches a perfect match under real Life. Results still landing.
